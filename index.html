TRANSFORM

MIT Media Lab | Tangible Media Group

Previous Next

In conjunction with PhD canidadates Daniel Leithinger and Sean Follmer, I contributed to the design and manufacturing of a shape-changing display called Transform. This is the second iteration of its kind, improving the manufacturability and scalability of the initial prototype, InForm. Specifically, I worked on redesigning parts for mass manufacturing and oversaw the production of these items by providing necessary technical drawings and quality control parts.

Wave Energy Device

Wedge Global

In conjunction with MIT Sloan Fellow and industrial engineer, Francisco Garcia Lorenzo, I analyzed Wedge Global’s wave energy device. More specifically, I evaluated their braking system to ensure safety and to try to reduce the cost of electricity by lowering the minimum required braking units. In addition to calcuations, I provided a list of recommended actions to try to reduce the number of brakes such as trying different materials and increasing the brake’s lever arm. Although not in the scope of the project, I also did simulations to evaluate the effect of the braking system on the braking rails.

Interstellar

SCPS Unlimited

Working with a creative design team, I helped with the backend creation of the movie Interstellar. Specfically, I worked with the transition between concept art and design for manufacturing. I helped with modeling parts from concept art for molding and 3d printing. I also helped with small electronics such as the cooling fans for the spacesuits.

Mercury

Delta Technologies

Previous Next

Working with a team of sixteen, I contributed to the design and manufacturing of a self morphing robot with flight and drive capabilities. This fully autonomous robot was designed for military applications and had several key features: active 3d maping of a room, sensor drops/localized radar, flight and drive mode, light and mobile. During this project, I was specifically working on the shroud/wheel system that integrates both a protective layer when in flight mode and a treaded system when in drive mode. I was also involved in the design behind each morph, which was very technically involved especially since we had to keep it simple and weight effective.

HER MAJESTY'S SHIP

Solar Cup

Previous Next

Working with a team of ten, I contributed to the design and manufacturing of a human controlled, solar powered boat. We implemented several unconventional features such as a flexible shaft, flexible solar panels, and a polycarbonate motor mount. We essentially reduced the overall weight of our boat while keeping up to part with power and efficiency. I was specifically in charge of the mechanical aspects of the boat such as the motor and flexible shaft. During the regional competition, I was also the skipper and point man for the team.
