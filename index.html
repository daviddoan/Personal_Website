TRANSFORM

MIT Media Lab | Tangible Media Group

In conjunction with PhD canidadates Daniel Leithinger and Sean Follmer, I contributed to the design and manufacturing of a shape-changing display called Transform. This is the second iteration of its kind, improving the manufacturability and scalability of the initial prototype, InForm. Specifically, I worked on redesigning parts for mass manufacturing and oversaw the production of these items by providing necessary technical drawings and quality control parts.

Wave Energy Converter

Wedge Global

In conjunction with MIT Sloan Fellow and industrial engineer, Francisco Garcia Lorenzo, I analyzed Wedge Global’s wave energy converter. More specifically, I evaluated their braking system to ensure safety and to try to reduce the cost of electricity by lowering the minimum required braking units. In addition to calcuations, I provided a list of recommended actions to try to reduce the number of brakes such as trying different materials and increasing the brake’s lever arm. Although not in the scope of the project, I also did simulations to evaluate the effect of the braking system on the braking rails.

Interstellar

SCPS Unlimited

Working with a creative design team, I helped with the backend creation of the movie Interstellar. Specfically, I worked with the transition between concept art and design for manufacturing. I helped with modeling parts from concept art for molding and 3d printing. I also helped with small electronics such as the cooling fans for the spacesuits.

Mercury

Delta Technologies

Working with a team of sixteen, I contributed to the design and manufacturing of a self morphing robot with flight and drive capabilities. This fully autonomous robot was designed for military applications and had several key features: active 3d maping of a room, sensor drops/localized radar, flight and drive mode, light and mobile. During this project, I was specifically working on the shroud/wheel system that integrates both a protective layer when in flight mode and a treaded system when in drive mode. I was also involved in the design behind each morph, which was very technically involved especially since we had to keep it simple and weight effective.

Lucky Ted 2.0

FIRST Robotics 687

Working with a team of ten people, I contributed in the design and fabrication of a human controlled robot for FIRST robotics. This robot was able to pick up full sized frisbees,shoot them approximately 20 feet consistently, and climb the first rung of the bonus pyramid. I was specifically responsible for the design and manufacturing of chassis and parts of the shooter.

Data Spectrum

Working with William Sinclair, the CEO of Data Spectrum, I helped in the design and quality testing of several different consulting projects. I was specifically involved in a project with Northrop Grumman that included troubleshooting, fixing, and redesigning their electronics box. I was also introduced to 3d modelling as I had to create electronic fixtures for a touch-screen medical display.
